Welcome to the Mother City

Cape Town rewards travellers who read the day right. In summer the "Cape Doctor" - a brisk south-easter - can sweep in and reshape your plans; it clears the air but makes exposed beaches and high hikes hard work. When you see cloud pouring over the top of Table Mountain like a "tablecloth", that's the south-easter at play: the cableway often closes, so pivot to a sheltered plan.

The golden rule: chase the calm, beat the heat, dodge the queues. Mornings are usually stillest and coolest; light winds and clear skies are your green light for the mountain, the Cape and the open beaches. Whatever the weather

Rainy-day winners

  • Two Oceans Aquarium & Zeitz MOCAA at the V&A
  • Company's Garden museums & the Castle of Good Hope
  • A Cape Malay cooking class in the Bo-Kaap
  • Constantia cellar tastings (indoors, fireside in winter)

Windy plan-B

  • Swap the high mountain for sheltered False Bay (Muizenberg, Boulders)
  • Head inland to the Winelands - valleys are calmer
  • Sea Point Promenade & harbour cafes stay enjoyable
  • Save Cape Point & Robben Island for a calmer day

Read the tablecloth

Cloud cascading over Table Mountain = strong south-easter. The cableway and Lion's Head get rough and may close. If the "tablecloth" is out, go low and sheltered today and save the summit for a clear, still morning.

How to dodge the crush

  • Table Mountain: pre-book the cableway online and take the first car (08:00) or the last light slot - midday queues are longest.
  • Go weekday, go early or late: attractions are calmest at opening and in the late afternoon.
  • V&A Waterfront: check whether a cruise ship is in port before you go - that's the difference between a stroll and a scrum.
  • Beat the traffic: avoid the N1/N2 at rush hour (approx 07:00-09:00 & 16:00-18:00).
  • Beaches: first-light or sunset beats midday for light, space and parking.
  • Eat early or late: book the 18:00 or the 21:00 table to skip the peak-hour wait.
  • Cape Point & Robben Island: pre-book entry/ferries; sail on calm days as wind cancels crossings.

Guest essentials

Getting around

  • Uber & Bolt are cheap, plentiful and the easiest way around.
  • MyCiTi buses cover the city, Waterfront, Camps Bay & the airport.
  • Driving is on the left; distances are bigger than they look.

Money & tipping

  • Tip ~10-15% in restaurants; a little for car guards & porters.
  • Cards are widely accepted; carry some cash for markets.

Stay safe & well

  • Keep valuables out of sight; use ride apps after dark.
  • The African sun is fierce - sunscreen, hat, water, even when breezy.
  • Tap water is safe to drink across the city.

Practical bits

  • Emergencies: 112 from any mobile, or 10111 (police).
  • SIM/data: grab a local prepaid SIM at the airport or a mall.
  • Plugs: Type M / N, 230V - bring an adapter.

Hidden gems

  • Sunrise at Lion's Head - beat the crowds and the heat for a 360-degree glow.
  • Oranjezicht City Farm Market (weekends) - local produce by the sea.
  • Llandudno sundown - a quieter, dramatic alternative to Camps Bay.
  • Scarborough & Misty Cliffs - wild, uncrowded coastline past Kommetjie.
  • The Foreshore & CBD street art - a self-guided wander between cafes.
  • Newlands Forest walk - shady, sheltered greenery when the wind blows.
