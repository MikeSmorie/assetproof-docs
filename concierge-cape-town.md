# AssetProof Concierge — Cape Town Knowledge Pack

> **What this is.** Local-intelligence reference for the AssetProof Concierge to reason over when helping guests in Cape Town and on the Cape Peninsula. It describes *types* of places, areas, weather logic, and "search-for-X" prompts — deliberately **evergreen**, so it stays accurate as individual venues open, close, rebrand, or change hours. When a guest wants a specific named place, current hours, prices, or "what's on tonight," hand them a **search prompt** (see the final section) rather than a stale name.
>
> **Tone for the concierge.** Warm, practical, street-smart. Give one clear recommendation plus a backup, factor in today's weather, and always respect the guest's interest and energy. Never invent venue names, prices, or opening times.

---

## 0. Quick reference (concierge cheat-sheet)

- **Currency:** South African Rand (ZAR / R). Cards widely accepted; carry some cash for tips, markets, parking attendants.
- **Tipping:** ~10–15% in restaurants; round up taxis; ~R5–R20 for car-guards and petrol attendants; ~R20–R50/bag for porters.
- **Tap water:** Safe to drink in Cape Town.
- **Plugs:** Type M / Type N, **230V**. (Type M is the large three round pins; newer sockets are Type N.) Bring an adapter.
- **Getting around:** Uber and Bolt everywhere and cheap; MyCiTi bus on main corridors; car hire best for the peninsula and winelands.
- **Emergencies:** **112** from any mobile (all-purpose), **10111** police, **10177** ambulance.
- **Weather rule of thumb:** Summer can be windy (the south-easter); winter is wetter but greener and quiet. Always check the **Table Mountain cableway status** before heading up.
- **Safety in one line:** Be city-street-smart — confident, low-profile with valuables, use ride apps after dark, don't walk isolated areas alone at night.

### Transport

- **Uber / Bolt:** The default for visitors. Cheap, reliable in the city, Atlantic Seaboard, Southern Suburbs and the Waterfront. Coverage thins out toward Cape Point and deep winelands — plan returns. Safer than street-hailing.
- **MyCiTi bus:** Clean, safe rapid-bus network on main routes (airport ↔ city, City ↔ Atlantic Seaboard/Sea Point/Camps Bay, Waterfront, etc.). Buy a **myconnect card** and load fare. Good for set corridors, not door-to-door.
- **Car hire:** Best (often essential) for the **peninsula loop, Cape Point, and the winelands**. South Africa drives on the **left**. Roads are good; watch for the N1/N2 at rush hour. Always park in attended/secure spots and leave nothing visible in the car.
- **Hop-on hop-off sightseeing bus:** Easy, low-stress overview for first-timers — loops the city icons (Table Mountain lower station, Camps Bay, Waterfront, Kirstenbosch, etc.) with commentary. Good on a clear day.
- **Metro rail / minibus taxis:** Used by locals; **not recommended** for visitors unfamiliar with the city.

### Safety basics (street-smart, not scared)

- **Day vs night:** Many areas pleasant by day become quiet/risky after dark. After dark, **use Uber/Bolt** rather than walking, especially away from busy strips.
- **Low profile:** Don't flash phones, jewellery, or cash. Keep bags zipped and in front in crowds.
- **Walk where there are people.** The Sea Point promenade, V&A Waterfront, Camps Bay strip and busy City Bowl streets are fine on foot by day. Empty streets, isolated trailheads and the lower slopes of the mountain at quiet times are not.
- **Hiking:** Never hike alone. Go in groups, on popular trails, in good weather, and tell someone your plan (see Hikes section).
- **Cars:** Lock doors while driving, windows up in stop-start traffic, valuables out of sight, park in attended lots.
- **ATMs:** Use ones inside malls/banks; decline "help" from strangers.
- **Cape Flats / townships:** Guided tours only. Don't self-navigate.

## 2. Weather logic (critical — read before recommending anything)

Cape Town weather is a *Mediterranean* climate, but the defining variable is **wind and mountain cloud**, not just rain. The concierge should always factor today's conditions into a recommendation.

### The south-easter — "the Cape Doctor" (summer wind)

- A strong south-east wind that blows mainly in **summer (roughly Nov–Mar)**, sometimes for days. Called "the Doctor" because it clears pollution and humidity.
- **Effects:** It can be ferocious on the Atlantic Seaboard, Cape Point and exposed beaches (Clifton/Camps Bay get sandblasted), and it often **closes the Table Mountain cableway**. It lays the "tablecloth" cloud over the mountain.
- **When it blows — what to do:**
  - **Go where it's sheltered:** the **False Bay side** (Muizenberg, Kalk Bay, Simon's Town, Boulders) is usually calmer than the Atlantic side. Constantia and the Southern Suburbs are more protected.
  - **Switch to indoors / low-exposure:** wine tasting, museums, aquarium, Zeitz MOCAA, Bo-Kaap walk, markets, spa.
  - **Skip:** the cableway, exposed ridge hikes (Lion's Head), open-boat trips, picnics on Atlantic beaches.
  - Forecasts: a "berg wind" (hot, dry, from the interior) is the opposite — calm and very warm.

### Table Mountain's "tablecloth" & cableway closures

- The flat cloud that pours over the mountain ("the tablecloth") signals **wind and/or low cloud** on top. When it's down, the summit is socked in with no view, and the **cableway often doesn't run**.
- **Always check cableway status before going up** (give the guest a search prompt). If it's closed or the mountain is covered, redirect to a lower viewpoint (Signal Hill, Lion's Head lower slopes) or an indoor plan.
- Best summit conditions: a clear, **low-wind morning**. Go early before cloud and crowds build.

### Seasons

| Season | Months | Character | Implications |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Summer** | Dec–Feb | Hot, dry, long days, **windy** (south-easter). Peak tourist season. | Beaches, sundowners, early-morning hikes; plan around wind; book everything ahead; high prices & crowds. |
| **Autumn (shoulder)** | Mar–Apr | Warm, calmer winds, settled — many locals' favourite. | **Sweet spot:** great hiking & wine weather, fewer crowds. |
| **Winter** | Jun–Aug | Cooler, **wettest** months (cold fronts, rain, some storms), green landscapes, big seas. Whale season building on False Bay. | Fewer crowds, lower prices, cosy indoor culture/food/wine; pack a rain layer; some boat trips cancel. |
| **Spring (shoulder)** | Sep–Nov | Warming, wildflowers, whales peak, generally pleasant. | **Sweet spot** with shoulder pricing; weather variable but improving. |

**Best months overall:** the shoulders — **Sep–Nov** and **Mar–Apr** — for the balance of decent weather, calmer wind, lower prices and thinner crowds.

## 3. Beat the crowds, queues & peak prices

### Peak calendar

- **Festive peak (busiest & priciest):** **mid-December to mid-January**, overlapping SA summer school holidays and international visitors. Beaches, the Waterfront, Cape Point and the cableway are packed; accommodation and car hire spike; book far ahead.
- **SA school holidays** (also shorter breaks around Easter/April and other terms) bump up domestic crowds at family spots and the peninsula.
- **Shoulder (the sweet spot):** **Sep–Nov** and **Mar–Apr** — good weather, lighter crowds, better value.
- **Winter (quiet & cheaper, wetter):** **Jun–Aug** — fewest tourists, lowest prices, best deals; plan for rain and have indoor backups.

### Concrete tactics (hand these to guests)

- **Table Mountain cableway:** **Pre-book online** to skip the ticket queue. Go at **opening** (first cars) or **late afternoon**; avoid 10:00–14:00. Check the **status/webcam** first — don't drive up into cloud or wind.
- **Go on weekdays.** Beaches, restaurants and attractions are calmer Mon–Thu than weekends.
- **Eat early or late.** Book popular restaurants ahead; arrive at opening or after the first sitting to dodge the rush.
- **Check for cruise ships before the V&A.** When ships are in port, the Waterfront and aquarium surge. (Search prompt below.)
- **Avoid the N1 / N2 at rush hour** (roughly 07:00–09:00 and 16:00–18:00). Time peninsula and airport runs around it.
- **First light or sunset beats midday.** Icons (Cape Point, Boulders, beaches, viewpoints) are emptier, cooler and more beautiful at the edges of the day.
- **Pre-book the big-ticket items:** Robben Island ferry, Table Mountain, popular wine-estate tastings/restaurants, and any boat trips — especially in peak season.
- **Sunrise hikes** (Lion's Head) beat the crowds and the heat — but only in good weather and never alone.

## 5. By interest, deeper

### Hikes (by effort — and a standing safety rule)

> **Safety rule for every hike:** Never hike alone. Go in a group, on popular routes, start early, check the weather (no high wind, no incoming cloud/rain), carry water, a layer and a charged phone, tell someone your route and return time, and turn back if conditions change. Mountain weather flips fast. For anything beyond a gentle stroll, consider a local guide.

- **Gentle (easy, family-friendly):**
  - **Sea Point / Mouille Point promenade** — flat seafront walk, any fitness.
  - **Company's Garden & city heritage walk** — flat, shaded, central.
  - **Kirstenbosch garden trails** incl. the **Tree Canopy Walkway ("Boomslang")** — easy, gorgeous.
  - **Green Point Urban Park** — flat, family.
- **Moderate (a proper climb, busy trails):**
  - **Lion's Head** — switchbacks with optional chain/ladder sections; sunrise/sunset classic; not in high wind.
  - **Signal Hill saddle walks** — easy-moderate viewpoint strolls.
  - **Kirstenbosch → Skeleton Gorge** (start of the climb up the back of Table Mountain) — shaded, steeper.
- **Strenuous (fit, sure-footed, ideally with a guide):**
  - **Platteklip Gorge** — the direct, steep, unrelenting route up Table Mountain's front face (you can take the cable car down).
  - **India Venster** — exposed, scrambly route up Table Mountain — for experienced hikers/guided only.
  - **Skeleton Gorge → Maclear's Beacon** — long traverse over the top.
- **Serious / multi-hour peninsula & ridge routes** — go **guided**; conditions and navigation matter.

### Food & wine (search prompts + areas, not named venues)

- **Where to eat by area:** City Bowl/Gardens (eclectic, trendy), Sea Point (diverse, casual-to-smart, strong ethnic mix), V&A Waterfront (harbour dining, pricier), Camps Bay (beach + sundowner dining), Kalk Bay & the South Peninsula (seafood, harbour cafés), Constantia & the winelands (estate restaurants, fine dining).
- **Cape signatures to seek out:** Cape Malay curries & bobotie, fresh line-fish and seafood, braai (barbecue), boerewors, koeksisters, melktert, and local wines (Constantia, Stellenbosch, Franschhoek).
- **Vegetarian / vegan:** Cape Town is very veg-friendly. Search prompt: *"best vegan restaurants in [Sea Point / City Bowl / Observatory] open now."* Sea Point, the City Bowl, Observatory and the Southern Suburbs have the densest options.
- **Halal:** Cape Town has a large Muslim community and strong halal options, especially around **Bo-Kaap, Sea Point, the City Bowl, and the southern/Cape Flats areas.** Search prompt: *"halal restaurants near [area] open now."* For Cape Malay food, look around Bo-Kaap.
- **Kosher:** Concentrated around the Southern Suburbs / Sea Point Jewish community. Search prompt: *"kosher restaurants Cape Town open now."*
- **Wine without driving:** Use a hired driver or a wine-tour operator; Franschhoek's **wine tram** lets you hop estates without a car; Constantia estates are a short, easy Uber from town.

### Beer / craft + chocolate / coffee

- **Craft beer:** Cape Town has a lively microbrewery scene (notably around the Southern Suburbs and city). Search prompt: *"craft breweries / taprooms near [area] open now."* Many do tasting flights.
- **Coffee:** Strong specialty-coffee culture in the City Bowl, Sea Point, Woodstock and Observatory. Search prompt: *"best specialty coffee near [area]."*
- **Chocolate / sweet:** Look for artisan chocolatiers and dessert spots in the City Bowl and Waterfront. Search prompt: *"chocolate shops / chocolatiers Cape Town."*

### Families

- Boulders penguins, Two Oceans Aquarium, V&A Waterfront (big wheel, play areas), Muizenberg (gentle surf + bathing boxes), Sea Point promenade & pools, Kirstenbosch (lawns + canopy walk), Hout Bay seal boat, hop-on bus. Rainy-day: aquarium, museums, planetarium, indoor play.

### Romance

- Sunset at Signal Hill / Camps Bay / Bloubergstrand; a Constantia wine lunch; cable car up Table Mountain at golden hour (weather permitting); a Kirstenbosch summer concert; a private peninsula drive; dinner on the Camps Bay strip or at a winelands estate.

### Culture & history (incl. apartheid history — handle respectfully)

- **Approach with respect and accuracy.** Apartheid history is recent and personal for many South Africans. Present these as places of remembrance and learning, not "attractions"; encourage guests to engage thoughtfully and support community-led tours.
- **Robben Island** — guided by former political prisoners; the island where Nelson Mandela and others were imprisoned.
- **District Six Museum** — commemorates the community forcibly removed under apartheid; centred on memory and restitution.
- **Bo-Kaap** — Cape Malay heritage, Islamic history, slavery and emancipation history; living community — be respectful with photos.
- **Township / Cape Flats cultural tours** — guided, community-based; history, music, food, social enterprise.
- **City heritage:** Company's Garden, the Castle of Good Hope, slave-history sites, the national museum and gallery cluster.

### Rainy-day winners

Two Oceans Aquarium · Zeitz MOCAA · District Six & city museums · indoor markets · winelands/Constantia tastings (cosy) · Bo-Kaap cooking experience · spa · V&A Waterfront (covered shopping/dining) · planetarium · coffee & bookshop crawl in the City Bowl.

### Windy-day plan-Bs (when the south-easter blows)

Head to the **False Bay side** (Muizenberg, Kalk Bay, Simon's Town, Boulders — usually sheltered) · go **inland** to the **winelands/Constantia** · pick **indoor culture** (museums, aquarium, Zeitz MOCAA) · walk **sheltered streets** (Bo-Kaap, City Bowl, Kalk Bay) · **skip** the cableway, exposed ridge hikes, Atlantic beaches and open-boat trips.

## 6. Practicalities

- **Tipping:** Restaurants ~**10–15%** (check it isn't already added for larger groups); taxis/Uber — round up; **car-guards** R5–R20; petrol attendants ~R5–R20; porters R20–R50/bag; tour guides at your discretion.
- **Money & cards:** Cards (incl. contactless) accepted almost everywhere; carry some **cash** for tips, car-guards, small markets and parking. ATMs inside malls/banks; decline stranger "help."
- **Water:** **Tap water is safe to drink** in Cape Town.
- **Sun safety:** UV is strong, especially in summer and at altitude/on the water — sunscreen, hat, water, even on breezy days (the wind hides the burn).
- **Plugs / power:** **Type M / Type N, 230V, 50Hz.** Bring a South Africa adapter (the large three-round-pin Type M is the classic one; newer Type N sockets are increasingly common).
- **SIM / data:** Local prepaid SIMs are cheap and widely sold (you'll need your passport to register, per RICA law). eSIMs work too. Coverage is good in the metro; patchier toward Cape Point and remote winelands.
- **Emergency numbers:** **112** from any mobile (general emergencies, routed to dispatch) · **10111** police (landline/all) · **10177** ambulance/medical. Save your accommodation's address and a local contact.
- **Driving:** Left-hand side; carry your licence (or international permit + home licence); toll on Chapman's Peak; secure parking and nothing visible in the car.

## 7. Good search prompts (for things that change — hand these out)

When a guest asks for anything live, named, time-sensitive, or fast-changing, give them a ready-to-use search prompt instead of a name that may be wrong:

- **Cableway status:** *"Table Mountain cableway operating status today"* (or check the official cableway site/webcam).
- **Live music tonight:** *"live music Cape Town tonight [date] near [area]"*
- **What's on this weekend:** *"things to do / events in Cape Town this weekend [dates]"*
- **Today's markets:** *"food markets open today Cape Town [day]"*
- **Cruise ships in port (V&A crowding):** *"cruise ship schedule Cape Town V&A Waterfront [date]"*
- **Tide times (tidal pools, rock pools, Sea Point/St James pools):** *"tide times Cape Town today"* / *"[tidal pool name] tide times"*
- **Surf / beach conditions:** *"Muizenberg surf report today"* / *"Cape Town wind forecast today"*
- **Chapman's Peak Drive open?:** *"Chapman's Peak Drive open status today"*
- **Whale season / sightings:** *"whale watching Cape Town / Hermanus this month"*
- **Restaurant open now + dietary:** *"[vegan / halal / kosher / seafood] restaurants near [area] open now"*
- **Wine tour / driver booking:** *"Cape winelands wine tour with driver [date]"*
- **Sunset time:** *"sunset time Cape Town today"* (to plan Signal Hill / Lion's Head / Camps Bay).
- **Weather + wind:** *"Cape Town weather and wind forecast [date]"* — the single most useful check before planning a day.
